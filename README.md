## Key Findings

**Budget:** Veterans Services has flatlined at $2.5–3.3M since FY2019 while combined DEI/equity departments went from $548K to $22.3M — a 40× increase. The Office of Workforce Development alone ($6.5M) is double the entire Veterans budget.

**Education:** BPS earned 0 out of 12 achievement points at the high school level. Every subject declined. Boston trails the state by 11–20 percentage points across every MCAS subject.

**ICE:** 5,100+ administrative arrests in Boston AOR (Jan–Oct 2025), roughly 3× year-over-year increase despite sanctuary city policies. Operation Patriot 2.0 alone accounted for 1,400+ arrests.

**Crime:** Massachusetts is #2 safest state for homicide (1.9 per 100K). Boston accounts for 19% of state violent crime with 9% of population. Robbery/assault rates run ~2× the national average; homicide is below. Black residents are 22% of Boston's population but 80% of homicide victims.

**Commercial RE:** Boston office vacancy hit 18.2% (up from <8% pre-COVID). Distressed sales closing at 31–62% discounts. Commercial property assessments declined for the first time in FY2026 (-0.6%), shifting tax burden to residential homeowners. CMBS office delinquency at 11.3%.

- **Crime:** FBI Crime Data Explorer (UCR/NIBRS), Boston Police Department
